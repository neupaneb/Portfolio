Soccer Stats - Soccer League Team Statistics Tracker

Description of my project:

The Soccer Statistics Tracker is a Java program designed to keep track of team statistics for a soccer league. It serves as a comprehensive tool for monitoring and analyzing the performance of soccer teams based on game scores reported throughout the season. The program reads data from a CSV file containing match details, such as team names and goals scored, and calculates various statistics, including wins, draws, losses, goals scored, goals conceded, and points earned. Additionally, it provides functionality to identify the best-performing team based on points earned and goal differentials, as well as high-scoring teams in the league.

Research on the topic of “Are Online Classes Better Way to Learn New Languages than Physical Classes.”

Description of my research paper:

In collaboration with Dr. Ruth E. Smith, Director of the School of Humanities, I conducted research into the effectiveness of language learning methods. Our study unveiled compelling insights, demonstrating the superiority of traditional physical classes over online platforms in fostering language acquisition. Through meticulous analysis and comparison, we elucidated the nuanced dynamics at play, showcasing the distinct advantages offered by in-person instruction in facilitating comprehensive language learning experiences. This research not only underscores the enduring value of traditional pedagogical approaches but also provides valuable guidance for educators and institutions seeking to optimize language learning outcomes in an increasingly digital landscape.
